Abstract

Existing symbolic music processing methods predominantly rely on token-based representations, which generate extremely long sequences and struggle to capture global structural patterns. We propose MusiCode, a framework combining representation learning, data mining, and vector quantization for music analysis. Our framework consists of three components: (1) a Transformer-based autoencoder learning compact 768-dimensional embeddings, (2) systematic data mining techniques including UMAP and HDBSCAN clustering, and (3) a theoretical framework for Residual Vector Quantization (RVQ).

We implement and validate the first two components on 83,362 music token embeddings. Clustering analysis reveals meaningful patterns with partial voice separation. Baseline comparison demonstrates HDBSCAN achieves 82% higher Silhouette Score than K-means by identifying density-based structures. Robustness analysis validates stability under 20% noise, parameter variations, and high reproducibility (ARI > 0.51).

MusiCode Framework

Overview: The Transformer-based autoencoder (Component 1) compresses token sequences into 768-dimensional embeddings. The learned embeddings are analyzed through parallel data mining techniques (Component 2): UMAP dimensionality reduction and HDBSCAN clustering. Component 3 provides a theoretical framework for residual vector quantization.

Methodology

Component 1: Transformer-Based Representation Learning

Architecture: 6-layer Transformer autoencoder with multi-head self-attention

  • Input: REMI-tokenized music sequences (vocabulary size: 2,048)
  • Embedding Dimension: 768-dimensional continuous vectors
  • Training: 100 epochs on 83,362 music segments (48 hours on NVIDIA A100)
  • Objective: Self-supervised reconstruction loss with label smoothing

Key Innovation: Instead of operating on lengthy token sequences (hundreds of tokens per segment), our autoencoder compresses them into compact fixed-dimensional embeddings while preserving musically meaningful information.

Component 2: Data Mining with UMAP + HDBSCAN

UMAP Dimensionality Reduction

Reduces 768D embeddings to 50D for clustering and 2D for visualization

  • Algorithm: Uniform Manifold Approximation and Projection
  • Advantages: Preserves both local and global structure
  • Complexity: O(N log N), efficient for large datasets
  • Parameters: n_neighbors=15, min_dist=0.1, metric='cosine'
HDBSCAN Clustering

Density-based clustering with automatic cluster number determination

  • Algorithm: Hierarchical Density-Based Spatial Clustering
  • Advantages: Arbitrary shapes, noise detection, no pre-specified K
  • Search Space: 360 parameter combinations explored
  • Result: 530 clusters discovered automatically

Why HDBSCAN over K-means?

Traditional K-means requires pre-specifying cluster numbers and assumes spherical shapes. HDBSCAN automatically discovers natural groupings of arbitrary shapes, identifies noise points, and provides a hierarchical view of cluster structure‚Äîcritical for complex music representations.

Component 3: Residual Vector Quantization (Theoretical Framework)

Goal: Convert continuous embeddings into hierarchical discrete codes

  • Multi-Layer Structure: 8 quantization layers, each with 1,024 codebook entries
  • Compression Ratio: >300√ó (80 bits vs 24,576 bits for float32 768D vector)
  • Hierarchical Encoding: Early layers capture coarse structure, later layers capture fine details
  • Applications: Efficient storage, symbolic manipulation, discrete generative models

Note: This component presents a complete theoretical framework with mathematical formulation. Implementation and empirical validation are reserved for future work.

What We Learned

1. Learned Representations Capture Structure:

The Transformer autoencoder successfully learns meaningful musical patterns without explicit supervision, as evidenced by the emergence of voice-separated clusters and hierarchical organization.

2. Density-Based Clustering is Essential:

HDBSCAN's 82% performance improvement over K-means demonstrates that music representations form complex, non-spherical structures requiring density-based methods.

3. Noise Detection is Feature, Not Bug:

The 45% noise ratio reflects genuine musical diversity‚Äîtransitional patterns between styles and unique segments that don't fit neat categories.
